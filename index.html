Understand the Circuits

A quick look at what each circuit does and where it's used

RC

RC Circuit (Resistor + Capacitor)

A resistor and capacitor connected together. When voltage is applied, the capacitor charges and discharges exponentially instead of instantly, giving the circuit its characteristic time delay.

  • Time constant τ = RC controls how fast it charges
  • Acts as a low-pass filter — passes low frequencies, blocks high ones
  • Used in timing circuits, signal smoothing, and power-supply filtering
RL

RL Circuit (Resistor + Inductor)

A resistor and inductor in series. The inductor resists sudden changes in current by storing energy in a magnetic field, so current rises and falls gradually rather than instantly.

  • Time constant τ = L / R controls current rise/fall
  • Opposes rapid current changes — useful for chokes and surge protection
  • Used in motor drives, relays, and inductive filter circuits
RLC

RLC Circuit (Resistor + Inductor + Capacitor)

Combines all three components, producing resonance — a frequency where the circuit responds most strongly. Above and below that point, the response drops off.

  • Resonant frequency f₀ = 1 / (2π√(LC))
  • Quality factor Q shows how sharp/selective the response is
  • Used in radio tuning, oscillators, and band-pass/band-stop filters
